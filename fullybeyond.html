Problem Statement

CSUF lacked a dedicated platform for students to collaborate, showcase their skills, and tackle real-world problems through UX design. While hackathons existed, they didn't focus on UX/UI design, and design-a-thons often lacked beginner-friendly learning environments.

Goal

To create a virtual design-a-thon event that fosters creativity, collaboration, and community while addressing real-world problems through UX/UI design.

We wanted to empower students to develop their confidence and enhance their design skills through a beginner-friendly environment.

Research & Planning Process

To address these challenges, we conducted extensive research and sought guidance from experienced design-a-thon organizers. Our team efforts included:

Attending Other Design-a-thons

    Participated in events hosted by other universities to learn best practices, identify participant pain points, and observe event structures.

Connecting with Organizers

    Consulted with experienced organizers from other universities to gain insights on securing sponsorships, structuring events, and navigating logistical challenges.

Drawing Inspiration

    Incorporated key features such as workshops, mentorship sessions, and streamlined submission processes inspired by successful designathons.

Collaborative Planning

    Formulated a tailored plan to create a beginner-friendly virtual designathon addressing CSUF's unique needs.

Challenges Faced

Meeting Initial Deadlines

    Early in the process, our team struggled to meet deadlines. I implemented a reward system that motivated accountability and encouraged teamwork, ultimately improving task completion.

Sponsorship Withdrawals

    The week of the event, all sponsors pulled out. I instinctually decided to cover the prize funding myself to maintain team morale. However, my team rallied together to share financial responsibility, demonstrating resilience and commitment to our vision.

Logistics Complexities

    We initially underestimated the challenges of onboarding participants across platforms (Devpost and Discord). I addressed this by consolidating processes, simplifying instructions, and providing updates to participants.

Participant Tracking

    When acceptances were released, I noticed an issue that the RSVP Forms and Discord onboarding did not originally require participants to provide a consistent first and last names, which would create confusion with participant verification. I immediately notified my team and they addressed it in the Discord server, asking participants to adjust server names to first names, ensuring a smoother participation process.

Lessons Learned

Marketing Overlook

  • A date discrepancy in participant submission deadlines caused confusion, resolved through clear communication and updates.

Team Communication Gaps

  • Transparency, timely communication is vital for smooth collaboration.

Sponsorship Challenges

  • Early outreach didn't secure enough support; a contingency plan is essential for future events.

Reflection

FullyBeyond wasn't just an event... it was a labor of love aimed at creating something our younger selves would have appreciated. From beginner-friendly workshops to ensuring participants found teams, every decision was meticulously made to empower others to grow and succeed.

This experience taught me the importance of trust, resilience, and adaptability. FullyBeyond wasn't just about building a platform; it was about creating opportunities for students to believe in themselves and their potential. The challenges we faced and overcame are a testament to the heart and dedication poured into this event.
